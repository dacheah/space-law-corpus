**Art. 1.** Space resources are capable of being appropriated.

**Art. 2.** (1) No person may explore or use space resources without holding a written mission authorisation from the minister(s) responsible for the economy and space activities (hereinafter "the ministers"). (2) No one may be authorised to carry out the activity referred to in paragraph 1 either under cover of another person or as an interposed person for the exercise of that activity. (3) The authorised operator may carry out the activity referred to in paragraph 1 only in accordance with the conditions of its authorisation and Luxembourg's international obligations. (4) This law does not apply to satellite communications, orbital positions or the use of frequency bands.

**Art. 3.** The authorisation is granted to an operator for a mission of exploration and use of space resources for commercial purposes, upon written request addressed to the ministers.

**Art. 4.** Authorisation for a mission may be granted only if the applicant is a public limited company (société anonyme), a partnership limited by shares (société en commandite par actions) or a private limited liability company (société à responsabilité limitée) under Luxembourg law, or a European company (société européenne) having its registered office in Luxembourg.

**Art. 5.** The authorisation is personal and non-transferable.

**Art. 6.** The application for authorisation must be accompanied by all information relevant to its assessment and by a mission programme.

**Art. 7.** (1) The authorisation is subject to proof of the existence in Luxembourg of the central administration and the registered office of the operator to be authorised, including the administrative and accounting structure. (2) The operator to be authorised must have robust financial, technical and legal procedures and arrangements by which the mission of exploration and use — including the commercialisation — of space resources is planned and implemented. It must also have a robust internal-governance framework comprising in particular a clear organisational structure with well-defined, transparent and consistent lines of responsibility, effective processes to identify, manage, monitor and report the risks to which it is or might be exposed, adequate internal-control mechanisms including sound administrative and accounting procedures, and control and security arrangements for its technical systems and applications. (3) The arrangements, processes, procedures and mechanisms referred to in this article shall be comprehensive and proportionate to the nature, scale and complexity of the risks inherent in the business model of the operator to be authorised and to the mission for which authorisation is sought.

**Art. 8.** (1) The authorisation is subject to communication to the ministers of the identity of the direct or indirect shareholders or members, natural or legal persons, holding a direct or indirect participation of at least 10 per cent of the capital or voting rights in the operator, and of the amount of those participations or, if that 10 per cent threshold is not reached, of the identity of the twenty largest shareholders or members. The authorisation is refused if, having regard to the need to ensure sound and prudent management, the quality of those shareholders or members is not satisfactory. (2) The notion of sound and prudent management is assessed in the light of the following criteria: (a) the professional standing of the operator to be authorised and of the shareholders and members referred to in paragraph 1; (b) the standing, knowledge, skills and experience of any member of the management body of the shareholders and members referred to in paragraph 1; (c) the financial soundness of the shareholders and members referred to in paragraph 1; (d) the existence of reasonable grounds to suspect that money laundering or terrorist financing, or an attempt thereof, is being or has been committed in connection with the envisaged exploration mission or use of space resources, or that such mission or use could increase the risk thereof. The standing of the members of the management body of the shareholders or members referred to in paragraph 1 is assessed under the terms of article 9, paragraph 1, second sentence.

**Art. 9.** (1) The authorisation is subject to the condition that the members of the operator's management body possess at all times the standing, knowledge, skills and experience necessary to exercise their duties. Standing is assessed on the basis of judicial records and of any evidence tending to establish that the persons concerned enjoy a good reputation and offer every guarantee of irreproachable conduct. (2) The persons responsible for managing the operator must be at least two and must be empowered to effectively determine the direction of the activity. They must possess adequate professional experience through having already carried out analogous activities at a high level of responsibility and autonomy in the space sector or a related sector. (3) Any change concerning the persons referred to in paragraph 1 must be communicated in advance to the ministers. The ministers may request all necessary information on the persons who may have to meet the legal conditions of standing or professional experience. The ministers shall oppose the proposed change if those persons do not enjoy adequate professional standing or experience, or if there are objective and demonstrable grounds to consider that the proposed change risks compromising sound and prudent management. (4) The grant of the authorisation entails, for the members of the management body, the obligation to notify the ministers spontaneously, in writing and in a complete, coherent and comprehensible form, of any change concerning the substantial information on which the ministers relied in examining the authorisation application.

**Art. 10.** (1) The application for authorisation must be accompanied by a risk assessment of the mission. It shall specify the coverage of those risks by the operator's own financial means, by an insurance policy from an insurance undertaking not belonging to the same group as the operator to be authorised, or by a guarantee from a credit institution not belonging to the same group as the operator to be authorised. (2) The authorisation is subject to the existence of financial resources appropriate to the risks associated with the mission.

**Art. 11.** (1) The authorisation is subject to the condition that the operator to be authorised entrusts the audit of its annual accounting documents to one or more approved statutory auditors (réviseurs d'entreprises agréés) who can demonstrate adequate professional experience. (2) Any change concerning the approved statutory auditors must be authorised in advance by the ministers. (3) The institution of commissioners able to form a supervisory board, provided for in the amended law of 10 August 1915 on commercial companies, applies to operators only in the cases where the law on commercial companies makes it mandatory, even where an approved auditor exists.

**Art. 12.** The authorisation describes the manner in which the operator to be authorised satisfies the conditions of articles 6 to 11, paragraph 1. It may also contain provisions on: (a) the activities to be carried out on or from the territory of the Grand Duchy; (b) the limits to which the mission could be subject; (c) the arrangements for supervising the mission; (d) the conditions serving to ensure compliance by the operator to be authorised with its obligations.

**Art. 13.** For each application for authorisation, a fee is set by the ministers to cover the administrative costs incurred in processing the application. This fee varies between 5,000 and 500,000 euros according to the complexity of the application and the volume of work. A Grand-Ducal regulation determines the procedure applicable to the levying of the fee.

**Art. 14.** (1) The authorisation is withdrawn if the conditions for its grant are no longer met. (2) The authorisation is withdrawn if the operator does not make use of it within thirty-six months of its grant, renounces it, or has ceased to carry on its activity during the last six months. (3) The authorisation is further withdrawn if it was obtained by means of false declarations or by any other irregular means.

**Art. 15.** The ministers are responsible for the continuous supervision of the missions for which an authorisation has been granted.

**Art. 16.** The operator who has obtained an authorisation for a mission is fully liable for damage caused in the course of the mission, including in the course of all preparatory works and duties.

**Art. 17.** Obtaining an authorisation for a mission does not dispense with the need to obtain any other required authorisations or approvals.

**Art. 18.** (1) Anyone who has contravened or attempted to contravene article 2 shall be punished by imprisonment of eight days to five years and a fine of 5,000 to 1,250,000 euros, or by only one of those penalties. (2) Anyone who has contravened or attempted to contravene, respectively, the provisions of articles 5, 9(3) first subparagraph, 11(1) or (2), or the terms of the authorisation, shall be punished by imprisonment of eight days to one year and a fine of 1,250 to 500,000 euros, or by only one of those penalties. (3) Without prejudice to paragraphs 1 and 2, the court seised may order the cessation of the operation contrary to the provisions of this law, subject to a penalty payment (astreinte) the maximum of which may not exceed 1,000,000 euros per day of infringement established.

*Enactment formula:* We order and command that this law be inserted in the Journal officiel of the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg to be executed and observed by all whom it concerns. — The Minister of the Economy, Étienne Schneider. Cabasson, 20 July 2017. Henri.
